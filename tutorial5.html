Working Capital & Efficiency Analysis

Master the key metrics for managing short-term finances and operational efficiency

üíº
Capital Employed
The total amount of capital used for operations and generating returns
Capital Employed =
Total Assets - Current Liabilities
Represents long-term capital invested in the business (equity + non-current liabilities)
üìà
ROCE
Return on Capital Employed - measures profitability of long-term investments
ROCE =
(EBIT / Capital Employed) √ó 100
Shows how efficiently capital is being used to generate operating profits
üíö
Working Capital
The difference between current assets and current liabilities
Working Capital =
Current Assets - Current Liabilities
Indicates short-term financial health and operational liquidity
üîÑ
AR Turnover
Accounts Receivable Turnover - measures how quickly cash is collected
AR Turnover =
Sales Revenue / Average AR
Higher ratios indicate efficient credit management
üì¶
Inventory Turnover
Measures how many times inventory is sold and replaced during a period
Inventory Turnover =
COGS / Average Inventory
Higher ratios suggest efficient inventory management
üìÖ
Days Ratios
Converts turnover ratios into time periods
Days = 365 / Turnover Ratio
Shows average collection period or inventory holding period
üí° Key Insight: Working capital management is crucial for entrepreneurial success. These ratios help ensure you have enough liquid resources to meet short-term obligations while operating efficiently. Poor working capital management is one of the leading causes of business failure.

What Working Capital Represents

Understanding Working Capital:
  • Definition: The funds available for day-to-day operations
  • Formula: Current Assets - Current Liabilities
  • Positive WC: More current assets than current liabilities (healthy)
  • Negative WC: More current liabilities than current assets (potential liquidity issues)
  • Importance: Ensures the company can meet short-term obligations and operate smoothly
  • Management: Balance between having enough liquidity and not tying up too much capital

Components of Working Capital

Current Assets (+)

  • Cash
  • Accounts Receivable
  • Inventory
  • Short-term investments
  • Prepaid expenses

Current Liabilities (‚àí)

  • Accounts Payable
  • Short-term debt
  • Accrued expenses
  • Unearned revenue
  • Current portion of long-term debt
üí° Entrepreneurial Insight: Effective working capital management is critical for business survival. Many profitable companies fail due to poor working capital management - they can't meet short-term obligations even though they're profitable on paper. This is often called "insolvency while profitable."
