Content Strategy Guidelines

Building a compelling narrative with strategic focus

✓ DO: Strategic Focus
  • Open with your unified recommendation immediately
  • Focus 70% of time on evaluating the two funding offers
  • Address the core trade-off: financial sustainability vs. mission integrity
  • Use financial analysis to support strategic arguments
  • Anticipate and pre-emptively address board concerns
  • Connect your recommendation to long-term company vision
  • Show how your choice ensures both survival and mission
✗ DON'T: Time Wasters
  • Summarize the case background for the first 5 minutes
  • Define basic financial terms the board already knows
  • Present the two offers without analysis or evaluation
  • Leave your recommendation ambiguous or unclear
  • Ignore the opposing perspective on your team or board
  • Focus solely on numbers without strategic implications
  • Present as if you're external consultants, not founders

Example Opening Scenarios

Strong Opening Example

"Board members, thank you for your time. We're here today with a unified recommendation that we believe is the only viable path for LingoLearn. After rigorous analysis, we recommend accepting the Future Forward Fund investment of $12 million. Over the next 15 minutes, we'll demonstrate why this path ensures both our financial sustainability and preserves the core mission that makes LingoLearn valuable in the first place."

Weak Opening Example (Avoid)

"Hello everyone. Today we're going to talk about LingoLearn, which is a language learning app founded three years ago by Maya and Leo. The company has 3.5 million users and offers freemium subscriptions. They've received two funding offers, and we'll explain what those are..."

Problems: Wastes time summarizing known information, no clear recommendation, no strategic focus, breaks character.

Delivery & Communication Excellence

Maintaining character and persuasive impact throughout

1

Character Consistency

You are the founders and leadership team, not consultants.

Language Matters

Use: "Our company," "Our users," "Our mission," "We built this," "We're fighting for"

Avoid: "The company should," "They could," "LingoLearn's founders believe," "Our analysis shows that LingoLearn..."

  • Show emotional investment in the decision
  • Speak with passion and conviction about the mission
  • Acknowledge internal tension between Maya and Leo's perspectives
  • Demonstrate that you've come together as a unified leadership team
2

Unified Voice

Present as a cohesive leadership team with a single, clear recommendation.

  • Ensure smooth transitions between speakers
  • Build on each other's points rather than introducing contradictions
  • Support fellow team members during Q&A
  • Avoid hedging or showing uncertainty about your recommendation
  • Demonstrate that all team members are aligned behind the chosen path
Warning Signs
If team members contradict each other, show uncertainty, or fail to support the unified recommendation, this significantly damages persuasiveness.
✓ DO: Professional Delivery
  • Maintain eye contact with the "board" (audience/camera)
  • Use confident body language and vocal tone
  • Practice transitions between speakers
  • Speak at appropriate pace—not too rushed
  • Use pauses for emphasis on key points
  • Refer to each other by role (Maya/CPO, Leo/CEO)
  • Show engagement when others are speaking
✗ DON'T: Delivery Pitfalls
  • Read directly from slides for extended periods
  • Use academic or overly formal language
  • Appear disengaged when teammates are speaking
  • Fumble transitions or contradict each other
  • Show uncertainty about your recommendation
  • Break character by referring to yourselves in third person
  • Apologize for time management or technical issues

Critical Analysis Framework

Demonstrating strategic thinking and analytical rigor

Moving Beyond Surface-Level Analysis

The board has already read the case. They know the numbers. What they need from you is critical evaluation of the strategic implications of each funding offer.

Surface-Level (Insufficient)

"Ignite Capital offers $25 million at a $60 million valuation, giving them 29.4% stake. This is more money than Future Forward's $12 million offer."

Strategic Analysis (Strong)

"While Ignite's $25 million provides 10 months more runway, their requirement to pivot to enterprise clients fundamentally changes our business model. This means abandoning our 3.5 million organic users who trust us, destroying the brand equity we've built, and entering a competitive B2B market where we have no relationships or proof of concept."

Key Analysis Dimensions to Address ▼

Financial Sustainability

  • Runway extension with each option
  • Path to profitability feasibility
  • Capital efficiency and burn rate implications
  • Revenue model viability under each scenario

Strategic Positioning

  • Market positioning changes
  • Competitive landscape in new vs. current market
  • Brand value and reputation implications
  • Long-term growth trajectory

Mission Alignment

  • Impact on core user community
  • Social mission preservation vs. compromise
  • Team motivation and retention
  • Founder values and vision realization

Risk Assessment

  • Execution risks of each path
  • Market entry barriers (if pivoting)
  • Investor expectation management
  • Downside protection and exit scenarios

Excellence Markers in Analysis
  • Apply module frameworks (not generic business concepts) to support your analysis
  • Reference external sources that strengthen your strategic argument
  • Identify second-order effects and long-term consequences beyond immediate financial impact
  • Demonstrate understanding of stakeholder interests and trade-offs
  • Show critical evaluation—acknowledge strengths and weaknesses of both options honestly

Peer Assessment Guidelines

Understanding the contribution evaluation process

Purpose of Peer Assessment

The peer assessment indicates to the marker if someone contributed significantly more or significantly less than the team average. It does not affect the overall quality grade of your presentation—only the distribution of marks within the team.

1
Significantly Lower Contribution
2
Slightly Lower Contribution
3
Average Team Contribution
4
Slightly Higher Contribution
5
Significantly Higher Contribution

How to Evaluate Contribution

Contribution Area Evidence of Strong Contribution Evidence of Weak Contribution
Research & Analysis Conducted thorough research, contributed substantive analytical insights, applied module frameworks Minimal research effort, relied on others' analysis, no independent contribution to strategic thinking
Content Development Drafted significant portions of presentation, refined arguments, created compelling narratives Did not contribute to slide development or content creation, minimal input on messaging
Role Performance Mastered assigned role, delivered confidently, contributed meaningfully during presentation Underprepared for role, weak delivery, minimal presence during presentation
Collaboration Attended all meetings, met deadlines, responsive to team communication, constructive feedback Missed meetings, late on deliverables, poor communication, unhelpful or absent during team work
Q&A Preparation Anticipated questions, prepared responses, contributed during practice sessions, handled questions well Did not prepare for Q&A, unable to answer questions in their domain, relied entirely on others
✓ Fair Assessment Practices
  • Evaluate objectively based on actual contribution
  • Consider both quality and quantity of work
  • Document specific examples if scoring below or above 3
  • Assess your own contribution honestly
  • Remember that different roles may require different effort levels
  • Focus on contribution to final output, not just time spent
✗ Unfair Assessment Practices
  • Score everyone at 5 to "help the team" without justification
  • Score based on personal relationships rather than contribution
  • Penalize someone for having a different opinion during discussions
  • Score without concrete evidence of contribution differences
  • Use peer assessment as retaliation for conflicts
  • Ignore significant contribution imbalances to avoid confrontation
Important Note on Comments

If you award someone a score other than 3 (including yourself), you must provide specific comments explaining why. This is especially important when scoring team members down. Vague or missing justifications undermine the credibility of peer assessments.

Example Justifications

Score 5 Example

"[Name] took initiative as Board Liaison, coordinating all team meetings, ensuring deadlines were met, and creating the first draft of our strategic framework. They also led our practice sessions and provided substantive feedback that improved everyone's delivery. Their contribution was significantly above average."

Score 3 Example

"[Name] performed their role as CMO effectively, attending all meetings, completing assigned sections on time, and delivering their portion competently. Their contribution was consistent with the team average."

Score 2 Example

"[Name] missed two key team meetings without notice, submitted their assigned analysis three days late, and did not participate in practice sessions. During the presentation, their delivery was underprepared. Their contribution was below the team average."
